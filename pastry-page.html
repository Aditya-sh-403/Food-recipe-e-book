A flaky, buttery pastry with a golden crust, filled with rich, velvety cream and a hint of vanilla. Every bite melts in your mouth, offering a delightful, sweet indulgence.

    Ingredients:
  • 1 sheet of frozen puff pastry (store-bought)
  • 1/4 cup fruit jam or preserves (such as raspberry, apricot, or strawberry)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for sprinkling)
    Instructions:
  • Thaw the Puff Pastry:
    Take the frozen puff pastry sheet out of the freezer and let it thaw at room temperature for about 20-30 minutes, or according to the package instructions. It should be soft but still cold.
  • Preheat the Oven:
    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat to prevent sticking.
  • Prepare the Pastry:
    Unfold the thawed puff pastry sheet on a clean surface. If it is rolled, gently roll it out with a rolling pin to smooth it out and make it slightly thinner (about 1/8 inch thick).
  • Cut and Fill:
    Using a knife or a pizza cutter, cut the pastry sheet into squares or rectangles, about 3 inches by 3 inches. You can cut it into larger or smaller pieces depending on your preference. Place a small teaspoon of fruit jam in the center of each square or rectangle. Be careful not to overfill, as the jam can ooze out during baking.
  • Fold and Seal:
    Fold each square or rectangle in half diagonally to form a triangle, or fold the edges in to create a small parcel or turnover shape. Press the edges together firmly to seal. You can use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch and to ensure they are sealed well.
  • Apply Egg Wash:
    Crack the egg into a small bowl and beat it lightly. Brush the egg wash over the top of each pastry. This gives the pastries a golden, shiny finish as they bake. If desired, sprinkle a little sugar on top of each pastry for extra sweetness and a crisp texture.
  • Bake:
    Place the prepared pastries on the lined baking sheet. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until they are puffed up and golden brown.
  • Cool and Serve:
    Allow the pastries to cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy them warm or at room temperature.

This easy puff pastry recipe is perfect for beginners and results in a delicious, sweet treat with minimal effort. Enjoy your homemade pastries!
