Making French fries at home is simple and fun, even for a complete beginner. Here’s a straightforward recipe to get you crispy, delicious fries every time:

    Ingredients:
  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil (like canola or sunflower)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Optional: pepper, paprika, garlic powder, or any other seasonings you like
    Equipment:
  • A large bowl
  • A sharp knife
  • A cutting board
  • A baking sheet
  • Paper towels
    Instructions:
  • Prepare the Potatoes:
    Start by washing the potatoes under cold water. You can peel them if you prefer, but leaving the skin on adds extra texture and flavor. On a cutting board, cut each potato into thin strips. Aim for about 1/4 inch thick to ensure even cooking.
  • Soak the Potatoes:
    Place the potato strips in a large bowl and cover them with cold water. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes. This step removes excess starch and helps the fries become crispier
  • Preheat Your Oven:
    While the potatoes are soaking, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  • Dry and Season:
    After soaking, drain the potatoes and pat them dry with paper towels. Removing excess moisture is key to getting crispy fries. In a large bowl, toss the dried potato strips with vegetable oil. Ensure they are well-coated but not swimming in oil.
  • Bake the Fries:
    Spread the potato strips in a single layer on a baking sheet. Make sure they are not crowded; this helps them crisp up evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes. Halfway through, flip the fries to ensure they cook evenly on all sides.
  • Season and Serve:
    Once the fries are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oven. While they’re still hot, sprinkle with salt and any other seasonings you like.
  • Enjoy:
    Serve your homemade fries hot with ketchup, mayo, or your favorite dipping sauce.

And that’s it! Homemade French fries are a tasty treat that’s easy to make and perfect for any occasion.
